The Issue with Self-Censorship

Self-censorship is the act of censoring or classifying one's own discourse. Typically, this is done out of fear of loss of job, loss of life, wrongful imprisonment, travel restrictions, etc. Self-censorship causes vital research and information to never become public. 

Why Collect Data

The sole intent of collecting data on self-censorship is to allow for more informed decisions on what to do when there is an academic in need of assistance. 

Security in Place

The security we have in place to ensure that all information you share with us goes as follows:
1. Verification of identity with a known, trusted source
2. Assurance that data collected is heavily scrutinized
3. All data collected is completely anonymous
